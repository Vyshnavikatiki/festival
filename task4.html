About the event

Makar Sankranti is one of the important festivals in India, which is dedicated to the Sun God and marks the end of winter. Also known as the harvest festival, Makar Sankranti marks the transit of the Sun into Makara Rashi (Capricorn constellation or zodiac). Makar Sankranti 2025 is on Tuesday, January 14th. It heralds the start of longer days, and the Sun moves northward, which is why this period is known as Uttarayan and is considered very auspicious. Makar Sankranti is celebrated according to the lunar calendar, so it falls almost on the same day every year. Auspicious Time for Worship, Donation, and Bathing on Makar Sankranti: Punyakaal: 09:03 AM to 05:46 PM on 14th January 2025 Maha Punyakaal: 09:03 AM to 10:48 AM These are considered the most auspicious times for worship, making donations, and performing sacred baths on Makar Sankranti.

BHOGI

On Bhogi, people discard old and derelict things and concentrate on new things causing change or transformation. At dawn, people light bonfires with logs of wood, other solid-fuels, and wooden furniture at home that are no longer useful. This marks the end of the year's accounts and the beginning of new accounts on the first day of the harvest on the following day. Lord Indra is worshipped during the Pongal festival for the blessing of rains.Students join their families and communities to light bonfires early in the morning, symbolizing the discarding of old, unwanted things and embracing a fresh start.They gather with friends to enjoy the warmth of the fire, sometimes singing traditional songs or playing music.Students participate in drawing colorful rangolis (kolams) using rice flour, natural powders, or synthetic colors outside their homes or in school premises

KANUMA

Kanuma is a festival that is celebrated on the third day of the four-day Sankranti festival. It is a day to honor cattle, which are considered to be a vital part of agriculture and society. Cattle like cows, bulls, and buffaloes are bathed and decorated with turmeric, kumkum, colorful paint, garlands, and bells.Kanuma is a significant part of the Sankranti festival, celebrated primarily in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. It falls the day after Makar Sankranti and focuses on honoring and showing gratitude to cattle, which are considered essential for agriculture and rural livelihoods. Students, especially those in rural areas or with agricultural backgrounds, celebrate Kanuma with great enthusiasm.Some host community feasts or potlucks, where everyone contributes and shares traditional dishes.
